Topic: Institutional Order Flow • Price Action Principles

Institutional Order Blocks (OB)

Within advanced structural market tracking, an institutional order block is defined as a highly specific price range where commercial capital allocation algorithms have concentrated major block orders. These ranges represent localized surges of high-volume capital injection.

  • Bearish Order Block: The final consecutive bullish candle preceding a sharp, high-momentum downward expansion that breaks established market structures.
  • Bullish Order Block: The final consecutive bearish candle preceding an aggressive, high-velocity upward expansion that clears major swing highs.

The Mechanics of Liquidity Voids & Fair Value Gaps (FVG)

When high-frequency trading algorithms push pricing rapidly away from an expansion center, it creates a temporary order book imbalance. A Fair Value Gap represents an inefficiency where only one side of the market auction (pure buy-side or pure sell-side liquidity) was filled, leaving an unfilled volume gap behind.

Because structural algorithms are programmed to resolve severe data delivery gaps, the price has a strong tendency to retrace back into these inefficiencies. This rebalancing phase matches remaining resting limit orders and updates the asset inventory before continuing its primary trend direction.
